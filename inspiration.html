Inspiration: Mental Stimulation

I have a strong leaning for logic, standards and order. Perhaps it shouldn’t have been so surprising that I feel so passionate about good UX. I tend to gravitate towards design and designers where simplicity and order are prioritized.

These designers don’t really influence me because of their visual style, instead I look up to all of them for their expertise and their contribution to educating and inspiring the design community.

Don Norman – the Godfather

Advocacy, philosophy, research, experience, common sense; this is why I look up to Don Norman.

There is such a practical wisdom present in Don’s approach to design. He doesn’t get lost in trends or fashion; he approaches design pragmatically, with a view that it can improve the lives of humans.

I have a tendency to search for the ‘perfect’ solution, Don reminds me that humans are gonna human.

"The problem with the designs of most engineers is that they are too logical. We have to accept human behavior the way it is, not the way we would wish it to be."

- Donald A. Norman, The Design of Everyday Things

Chris Coyier – the Educator

Chris is the founder of CSS Tricks. I remember discovering this site early in my career and being in awe of what Chris knew and what he could do. He strives to better the design community and focuses on practical HTML and CSS for designers.

Chris's "Complete Guide to Flexbox" (from 2013!) is bookmarked in my browser and still a page that I refer back to.

Jeffrey Zeldman – the Evangelist

Zeldman’s book “Designing with web standards” (3rd Edition) was one of the first books about HTML and CSS that I ever purchased. It’s still on my bookshelf today - even though most of its content is now out of date - as a reminder of where I began.

I like that he works from an intellectual space and strives to make visible the hidden bits of design and development and working on the web.
